Traditions

During Lunar New Years, it is customary for people to dress in bright clothing, typically the color red. It is associated with good luck and fortune, hence why red is generally associated with Lunar New Years. Another important tradition is to spend time with family. During New Years Eve, families gather together for a final dinner. Even after the New Year festivities, people take a week off to visit family members they don't see very often. One common activity is giving or receiving red envelopes or 红包 (hong bao). Children receive envelopes with random amounts of money inside. Other activities include a fireworks show, new years cleaning, and lion dance parades

In China, there are 12 different animals which are representative of the 12 zodiac animals of the Lunar Calendar. A person's zodiac sign is determined by the year in which they were born. For example, children born between 02/12/2021 (Lunar New Years) and 02/01/2022 inherit the sign of the Ox. Much like the Aries or the Taurus familiar to us, each of these animals are related to a certain behavior or specific traits. For example, the Ox is known for its strength and determination. Some zodiac signs are considered much luckier than others. Some would consider those with a dragon sign to be more fortunate than others. However, this is no more than an idea that some people have. Food

Traditional foods during Lunar New Years include fish, oranges, noodles, and many others. Fish is eaten normally on a daily basis. However, it still is symbolic for prosperity. Noodles are typically eaten because they symbolize longitivity. Oranges are eaten because they represent good luck. However, some families don't eat them and prefer to use them as a house decoration.
